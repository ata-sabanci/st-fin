The Combinatorial Challenge

Every time a strategy is configured by hand, the trader selects one point in a vast space and hopes it's a good one — without visibility into what the rest of the space looks like. The individual tools are simple. The complexity lives in combinations.

The human mind excels at pattern recognition, but it is not built to systematically explore a space measured in tens of trillions of possibilities. This is where artificial intelligence changes the equation — not by replacing judgment, but by navigating the space that no human can explore manually.

📊 CSV Export — Portable Numerical Data

For deeper, more structured review, the same lens data can be exported from TradingView as a CSV file. When ST-Fin is applied to a chart, each enabled lens appears as a named data column in the exported file alongside timestamp and price data.

Important: Only active lenses are exported. If a lens is turned off in settings, it won't appear in the CSV. Before exporting, activate the specific lenses you want to review.

Recommended Workflow

1

2

Use TradingView's data export feature to save the chart data as CSV.

3

Review as Numeric Series

Study how the lens behaves near local highs, rejection zones, or failed breakouts.

4

Compare Against Chart

Map the findings back to the price chart, then repeat with another lens.

5

Combine Selected Lenses

Once individual behaviors show meaningful structure, combine them for multi-lens analysis.
