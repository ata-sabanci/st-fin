How It Works

The Geometric Framework

From raw price data to dimensionless geometric measurements. Three building blocks that make angles and areas comparable across any symbol, timeframe, or volatility regime.

📐 ICS — Isotropic Coordinate System

On a trading chart, time moves horizontally and price moves vertically — but they don't share the same unit. The same price movement can appear steeper or flatter depending on zoom, scaling, or timeframe. This makes raw visual angles inconsistent.

The ICS framework solves this by making both axes dimensionless:

Vertical axis: y = log(price) / σ — where σ is the Yang-Zhang volatility estimate
Horizontal axis: x = bars / lookback — normalized time displacement

Inside this normalized space, triangle measurements are calculated mathematically, not visually. Price displacement becomes dy, time displacement becomes dx, the angle comes from atan(dy/dx), and area from 0.5 × |dy| × |dx|.

This is what makes ICS triangles different from ordinary chart drawings: they are calculated structures, not visual impressions.

🧊 Frozen Anchors — The Fixed Reference

Before any triangle can be measured, the model needs a fixed origin — a point that does not move while the market continues around it. Without a stable reference, geometric measurements would shift every bar.

The strategy scans a lookback window (default: 23 bars) and identifies three structural levels:

Ceiling

Highest High in the lookback window

Center

Geometric mean √(HH × LL)

Floor

Lowest Low in the lookback window

These levels are frozen at the bar where the scan completes. From that moment, the anchor holds still. When the close breaks above the ceiling or below the floor, the anchor resets and a new cycle begins.

Frozen anchor levels on a daily chart. Ceiling, Floor, and Center are locked at the freeze bar and held fixed. When the close breaks the ceiling or floor, the anchor resets.

Why 23? The default lookback of 23 is a prime number near a typical trading month length, but not directly aligned with common cycle lengths (5, 10, 14, 20, 21). This reduces harmonic overlap. The period is fully adjustable.

Five Triangle Types

Each Shape Measures Something Different

Three main right triangles track price relative to the upper, middle, and lower reference. Two pin triangles isolate the candle's shadow behavior — upper and lower wick rejection.

▲ Ceiling — HH → High

Connects the frozen highest high to the current bar's high. Tracks how the market's upper boundary behaves relative to the structural ceiling. When the high sits below the frozen ceiling, the angle is negative — price is compressing beneath the reference.

◆ Center — Mid → Mid

Connects the frozen geometric mean to the current bar's geometric mean (√(High × Low)). Captures the general directional lean — whether price favors the upper or lower half of the frozen range. Both endpoints use geometric mean for proportional weighting.

▼ Floor — LL → Low

Connects the frozen lowest low to the current bar's low. Tracks support behavior, compression near the lower boundary, or gradual lift away from the structural low. Mirrors the Ceiling triangle from the opposite direction.

△ Pin Up — HH → Upper Wick

Isolates the upper wick relative to the frozen ceiling. Three vertices form a non-right triangle: frozen HH (A), current bar's high (B), and body top (C). The angle at A captures how much the wick fans out as seen from the ceiling — quantified rejection behavior.

▽ Pin Down — LL → Lower Wick

Mirrors Pin Up from the floor. Captures lower wick rejection and buying absorption. When the lower wick is long near the floor, the geometry shows a measurable rejection from the lower zone.

Four Metrics

What Each Triangle Produces

θ Angle

Steepness of movement. How steeply price has moved relative to the reference, in degrees. Positive = above anchor, negative = below. Compresses naturally as time passes.

dy Distance

Signed displacement. How far the target has moved from the anchor in ICS space. Divided by sigma, so the same value represents the same relative stretch regardless of symbol.

A Area

Combined displacement. Product of time and price dimensions. Grows when either grows. Useful for assessing overall weight — a large area means the triangle occupies significant geometric space.

C̄ Centroid

Geometric balance point. Where the triangle's internal weight is concentrated. Shifts in centroid can highlight changes in structural balance before they become visible in angle or distance.
