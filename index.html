The Idea

What if price movement could be measured with geometry?

Instead of viewing price as a single line, ST-Fin examines market behavior through triangular measurements: angle, distance, area, and centroid. The triangle is not decorative — it is a structured measurement method that translates price movement into geometric relationships you can test, tune, and explore.

Five triangle types drawn on a daily chart. Each connects a frozen reference level to a specific point on the current candle, producing four independent geometric metrics.
Core Architecture

How It Works — In Three Steps

📐

1. ICS Normalization

Both chart axes are made dimensionless. Vertical: log(price) / σ (Yang-Zhang volatility). Horizontal: bars / lookback. This makes angles and areas comparable across symbols, timeframes, and volatility.

🧊

2. Frozen Anchors

The strategy scans a lookback window and freezes three levels: Ceiling (highest high), Floor (lowest low), Center (geometric mean). These stay fixed while price moves around them — a stable origin for measurement.

📊

3. Five Triangles × Four Metrics

Five triangles are constructed from the frozen anchors to the current bar. Each produces angle, distance, area, and centroid — 20 geometric lenses that evolve continuously, bar by bar.

Five Perspectives

Each Triangle Tells a Different Story

Three main triangles track how price moves relative to the upper, middle, and lower reference levels. Two pin triangles isolate the candle's wick behavior — rejection patterns that often carry structural meaning.

▲

Ceiling (HH → High)

Tracks how the market's upper boundary behaves relative to the structural ceiling. Compression toward zero signals proximity to the reference.

◆

Center (Mid → Mid)

Measures the directional lean — whether price favors the upper or lower half of the frozen structure. Uses geometric mean for proportional weighting.

▼

Floor (LL → Low)

Tracks support behavior, compression near the lower boundary, or gradual lift away from the structural low.

△

Pin Up (HH → Wick)

Isolates upper wick rejection. A long upper shadow near the ceiling produces a geometric spike — quantified rejection from the upper zone.

▽

Pin Down (LL → Wick)

Captures lower wick absorption. Measures buying pressure at the bottom of candles relative to the frozen floor.
