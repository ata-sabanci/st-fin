The 20 Geometric Lenses

Four metrics applied to five triangles. Each lens is a raw, unfiltered measurement plotted directly — no smoothing, no reshaping. What you see is what the triangle produced.

▲ Ceiling ◆ Center ▼ Floor △ Pin Up ▽ Pin Down

▲ Ceiling Lenses

01 Ceiling Angle

Tracks the ICS angle between the frozen ceiling and the current bar's high. Negative when the high sits below the ceiling. When the reading reaches the boundary of its range, notable changes in price behavior tend to follow.

Ceiling Angle on SOLUSD daily. The angle moves in a negative range. When it reaches the boundary, price behavior shifts at the marked points.

02 Ceiling Distance

Signed displacement between the frozen ceiling and the current high in ICS space. Negative when the high is below the ceiling, approaching zero as the high nears the ceiling level.

Ceiling Distance on AAPL daily. Boundary extremes align with visible shifts in price behavior.

03 Ceiling Area

Combines time and price displacement into a single non-negative reading. Expands as the triangle grows, contracts when vertical displacement narrows faster than horizontal extends.

Ceiling Area on daily chart. Area builds gradually and contracts when the vertical displacement narrows.

04 Ceiling Centroid

The geometric balance point of the Ceiling triangle. Shifts in centroid often lead visible price turns. The same numeric level can produce different outcomes — demonstrating that context matters.

Ceiling Centroid reaching zero at two separate points — different outcomes from the same level.

◆ Center Lenses

05 Center Angle

Measures the angle between the frozen center and the current bar's midpoint. Crosses zero as the midpoint moves above or below the structural center. When it reaches the boundary of its range, notable price shifts tend to follow.

Center Angle crossing zero and reaching the upper boundary of its range.

06 Center Distance

Signed displacement from the frozen center to the current midpoint. Shows how far the midpoint has stretched from the center.

Center Distance (cyan) and Ceiling Distance (red) read together — displacement from both anchors simultaneously.

07 Center Area

Geometric footprint of the Center triangle. Expands as the move develops, peaks, then contracts — tracking the weight and maturity of the current structure.

Center Area reaching peak before contracting. Direction changes align with price shifts.

08 Center Centroid

Tracks where the Center triangle's balance is concentrated. Remains stable within cycles, drops sharply at anchor resets — raw centroid changes, not smoothed signals.

Center Centroid — stable within cycles, sharp drops at anchor resets.

▼ Floor Lenses

09 Floor Angle

Slope between the frozen floor and the current bar's low. Stays positive while price holds above the floor. Sharp drops mark anchor resets, not ordinary bearish signals.

10 Floor Distance

Pure vertical separation from the frozen floor, removing the time-slope component. More directly tied to the lower-side price relationship.

11 Floor Area

Geometric footprint from the floor side. Read alongside Ceiling Area to see whether geometric weight builds on one side or both simultaneously.

Floor Area (cyan) and Ceiling Area (red) expanding in parallel — geometric weight on both sides.

12 Floor Centroid

Balance point of the Floor triangle. Read together with Center and Ceiling centroids to see how balance is distributed across the whole structure.

Three centroids — Floor, Center, Ceiling — tightly clustered and broadly parallel. Drops mark anchor resets.

△ Pin Up Lenses

13 Pin Up Angle

Pulse-like behavior — flat near zero most of the time, spiking sharply on bars with geometrically significant upper wicks. Unlike main lenses, this one is event-driven.

14 Pin Up Distance

ICS-normalized length of the upper wick itself. Spends most time in a low range near zero. Rare excursions into the upper zone mark candles with notably long upper shadows.

15 Pin Up Area

When this reading stays flat near zero for an extended stretch, bar after bar produces no meaningful upper shadow. That sustained silence — the absence of upper-wick rejection — often coincides with a clean directional move.

16 Pin Up Centroid

Runs flat during sustained moves, with brief pulses marking bars where the wick geometry changed — potential points of interest along a trend.

▽ Pin Down Lenses

17 Pin Down Angle

Rare deep negative spikes — reaching -80 to -90 degrees — marking geometrically rare lower-wick events. Over eighteen months, only a few such spikes appear.

18 Pin Down Distance

Continuously oscillating — the normal rhythm of lower-wick lengths bar after bar. The outlier drops that break below the usual boundary mark abnormally long lower shadows.

19 Pin Down Area

Gains meaning primarily through combination. When Floor Area converges toward Pin Down Area, the structure is squeezed near the floor — a convergence that often aligns with visible shifts.

20 Pin Down Centroid

Each sharp pulse aligns with a discrete jump on the price chart. The sequence of pulses traces the step-by-step rhythm of the market's advance — a geometric mirror of the market's own cadence.

Reading lenses together adds a second layer of observation. A single lens describes one geometric relationship. When two or more lenses are placed on the same pane, their interaction — convergence, divergence, crossing — reveals patterns that no single lens shows on its own. The same raw data, observed as a set of relationships.

Ceiling Angle (red) and Center Angle (cyan) read together — convergence points align with price shifts.
